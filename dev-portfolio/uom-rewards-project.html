Summary

UOM Rewards: A Gamified System That Helps College Students Stay on Track While Staying Connected

Around 63% of college students not involved in extracurriculars find it difficult to make friends, according to Inside Higher Ed. UOM Rewards aims to change that by using gamification to encourage campus involvement, improve time management, and foster meaningful connections.

How did we get to UOM Rewards?

Research → Synthesis → Ideation → Prototyping → Testing

We interviewed six University of Memphis students to gauge the core cause of their problem. We recruited different types of students from club members, transfer students, and ex-club members. From there, we synthesized these interviews into three personas.

The Club Member,
The Transfer Student,
and The Ex-Club Member

The Club Member is looking for a way to better manage her time while also encouraging other students to join her club.

The Transfer Student is new on campus and is looking to make some new friends and become more involved on campus.

The Ex-Club Member is looking for an easier way to find campus events that aligns with their busy schedule and connect with some friends--old and new.

From Insights to Features

We arrived at the idea of UOM Rewards from our users being overwhelmingly interested in that idea from our storyboards and speed date sessions.

Key Learnings

What We Would Improve

  1. We could've explored adding more social features to the app.

    2. We assumed that our users would take social matters into their own hands, but for people who are more introverted or anxious, that is not a good solution for them.

  2. Our rewards system could've been more clear.

    3. Some users were actually unclear about the rewards process during testing. Now that I have more design knowledge, I would add a point system instead of a progress bar.
